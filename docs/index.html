Two-way sync between Obsidian and Notion

Write in Obsidian. Share in Notion. Stop copy-pasting between them. Notional is a free, open-source Obsidian plugin that pushes and pulls your Markdown notes to Notion — links intact, structure preserved, nothing overwritten by surprise.

Why sync Obsidian with Notion?

You think in Obsidian — it is fast, private, and yours. But your clients, teammates, and shared wikis live in Notion. Copy-pasting between them breaks your [[wiki-links]], flattens nested outlines, and leaves you with stale duplicates. Notional ends the copy-paste tax: one command and your note lands in Notion looking the way it should.

Features

🔁 True two-way sync

Push notes to Notion and pull teammates' edits back into Obsidian. Sync picks the right direction automatically.

🔗 Wiki-links → page mentions

Your [[internal links]] become real, clickable Notion page mentions — not dead plain text.

🛟 Never overwrites silently

If both sides changed, Notional stops and asks which version to keep. No surprise data loss, ever.

🔐 Private by design

One-click OAuth connect. Your token stays on your device and talks only to Notion — there is no server in the middle.

📁 Folder publishing

Push a single note or a whole project folder at once, with scoped control over exactly what goes public.

🧩 Rich block support

Headings, checklists, tables, callouts, toggles, code, and deep nesting are preserved both ways.

How to install

  1. In Obsidian, open Settings → Community plugins → Browse and search “Notional”.
  2. Click Install, then Enable.
  3. Open Settings → Notional, paste a Notion API token (or use one-click Connect with Notion), and create your notes database.
  4. Open any note and hit Push. Done.

Full setup guide on the GitHub README.

Frequently asked questions

How do I sync Obsidian with Notion?

Install the Notional plugin from Obsidian's community plugin browser, connect your Notion workspace with an API token or one-click OAuth, then use the Push, Pull, and Sync commands on any note. Notional handles Markdown-to-Notion conversion and keeps both sides in sync.

Is Notional free and open source?

Yes. Notional is completely free and released under the GNU GPL v3.0. The full source is on GitHub.

Does it do two-way sync, or just export?

True two-way sync. You can push Markdown notes to Notion and pull Notion edits back into Obsidian. When both sides change, Notional asks you which version to keep rather than guessing.

Is my data private?

Yes. Notional runs entirely on your device. Your notes and Notion token never touch a third-party server — they go directly from your machine to the Notion API. See the privacy policy.

What happens to my wiki-links and nested notes?

[[wiki-links]] are converted into clickable Notion page mentions, and deeply nested outlines are uploaded past Notion's per-call block limit — so your structure survives the trip.
